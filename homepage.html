Background

Impressionism emerged in the late 1800s in France, marking a big shift in the art world. This movement is characterized by a radical departure from traditional painting techniques, embracing a more vibrant portrayal of the world. Impressionists sought to capture the fleeting effects of light and colour, rendering scenes with rapid brushstrokes and a remarkable attention to the atmospheric conditions.


The term 'Impressionist' was first used as an insult in response to an exhibition of new paintings in Paris in 1874. A diverse group of painters, rejected by the art establishment, defiantly set up their own exhibition. They included Monet, Renoir, Pissarro and Degas.

“The essence of Impressionism was to reflect the reality of life through the artist's immediate perception, often painted en plein air (outdoors) to directly engage with the changing environment.”

-Anonymous

Characteristics

The Impressionist movement was marked by a set of distinctive characteristics that represented a radical change in the way art is made and understood.

Colour & Light

Impressionist used bright colours and aimed to capture the fleeting effects of light on the environment . They preferred to place complementary colours next to each other rather than mixing them, creating an optical ‘blending’ effect.


“Haystacks” is a stunning example of this iconic interplay between colour and light.

Technique

To capture the temporary effect of light, impressionists used quick, loose brushstrokes. They applied a painting technique called impasto where paint was applied thickly onto the canvas, creating a textured appearance.


The famous “Impression Sunrise” by Monet is a clear example of this free application of brushstrokes.

Subject

Impressionist moved away from the mythical and religious themes of preceding movements, and preferred to focus on everyday subjects, for example:


  • Landscapes
  • The effect of light
  • Contemporary life
  • Urban and suburban life

Contemporary Example

Impressionism has evolved since the original movement and lives on through contemporary artists like Erin Hanson, who is one of the most famous modern impressionists.
